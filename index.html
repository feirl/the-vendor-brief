The Series

What exactly is
The Vendor Brief?

What's really going on underneath — the thing most brands don't see

Where most brands get it wrong — and why

What the brands who are winning are doing differently

Thought leadership, not a product pitch.

Each session takes one topic that's costing FMCG brands money on Amazon and unpacks it with depth, honesty, and operational insight. The purpose is to give value first, show deep understanding, and build trust with the people who make the decision to invest in this channel.

The Format

How it works

45-Minute LinkedIn Live

One key Amazon FMCG topic each month — specific enough to attract, broad enough to resonate

Nick Comer and Sophie Smith host — the expert voices, the points of view

One rotating guest per episode — an FMCG brand leader, Amazon insider, or category specialist

Brendan produces the first 2–3 episodes, with a structured handover to the internal team

Primary audience: UK FMCG brand decision-makers, with secondary reach to Amazon internal stakeholders

Tone

Honest, direct, insider.

Not polished corporate content. The kind of conversation you'd have at a trade show, on the record.

Monthly Cycle

The 4-Week Cadence

Week 1

Pick the Angle

Choose the one Amazon FMCG topic brands care about most this month. Lock the headline, confirm the guest, agree the core point of view. Identify the tension.

Week 2

Build & Promote

Create the LinkedIn event page. Push value-first promotion. Personal outreach divided amongst the senior team — reaching all clients and target prospects.

Week 3

Run the Anchor Event

Nick, Sophie, and the guest lead the point of view. 4–5 structured questions prepared in advance. Focus on education, not selling. One clear takeaway.

Week 4

Repurpose & Convert

Turn the session into 2–3 short clips, one quote card, one summary article, and a follow-up email. Warm leads get a personal follow-up within 48 hours.
